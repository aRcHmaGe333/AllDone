# Regulatory And Food Safety Checklist

Status: approved for pilot planning

Purpose
-------
This document defines the first review checklist for pilot one.

It closes `P3.4`.

## Food-contact materials

- confirm suitability of borosilicate glass for intended food-contact use
- confirm suitability of stainless lid materials
- confirm suitability of platinum-cured silicone for intended contact and wash conditions
- keep material documentation from suppliers once sourcing begins

## Wash and sanitation

- define wash temperature range
- define detergent and rinse chemistry assumptions
- define inspection requirements before release to refill-ready inventory
- define handling for contaminated or suspicious returns

## Labeling and traceability

- identify pilot labeling requirements by product category
- ensure container SKU traceability
- ensure batch traceability for filled goods where required
- ensure route and return records can be reconciled

## Tamper evidence

- identify which pilot categories need tamper-evident treatment
- dose-module closures should allow tamper band or equivalent where category requires it

## Scope control

Keep pilot one focused on categories that simplify compliance:

- grocery staples
- fridge-stable recurring goods
- tightly defined prepared basics

Avoid expanding pilot one into categories that create unnecessary regulatory complexity.

## Site and operating permission

- confirm building or site acceptance for scheduled recurring delivery and pickup
- confirm depot or wash-site handling assumptions are acceptable for the intended operation

## What remains external to this checklist

This document is a project checklist, not legal advice.

Before live pilot launch, a real compliance review should still confirm:

- local food-safety requirements
- local sanitation requirements
- any hygiene or cosmetics requirements if those categories are later added
