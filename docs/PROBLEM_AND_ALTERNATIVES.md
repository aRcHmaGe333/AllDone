# Problem And Alternatives

Status: active

## Company purpose sentence

AllDone is a closed-loop home provisioning system that replaces repetitive household shopping for recurring goods with reusable containers, return logistics, industrial washing, and recirculation.

## Buyer and user

### Default early buyer

- the household itself

### Default early user

- the person in the household who currently manages recurring shopping, replenishment, and household stock

### Early access partner

- building manager
- housing cooperative
- dense residential operator

These partners are likely to help with pilot access and density, even if they are not the direct payer in the first version.

## The problem

Recurring household supply is still handled through an inefficient combination of:

- repeated shopping trips
- disposable packaging
- fragmented deliveries
- overbuying and underuse
- repeated cognitive effort for the same needs

The problem is not that grocery delivery is too slow. The problem is that the retail loop itself is wasteful and repetitive.

## Top painful outcomes today

- people repeatedly spend time on the same low-value shopping task
- homes accumulate disposable packaging and poorly sized product units
- buying decisions are badly matched to actual usage, creating waste and stockouts

## Current alternatives

Today the pain is handled through:

- in-person supermarket shopping
- standard grocery delivery
- meal kits
- bulk purchasing and home stockpiling
- ad hoc subscription products for narrow categories

## Why current alternatives are inadequate

### In-person shopping

- high time cost
- repeated transport burden
- repeated cognitive load

### Standard grocery delivery

- still largely disposable-packaging based
- still item-picking based rather than system-based
- often poor at aligning delivered quantity with real household use

### Meal kits

- narrow use case
- limited flexibility
- often expensive and still packaging-heavy

### Bulk buying

- shifts waste into the home
- increases storage burden
- works poorly for freshness-sensitive goods

### Narrow subscriptions

- solve one category at a time
- do not remove the broader household chore

## Initial wedge market

The first wedge should be:

- dense residential households
- recurring staple consumption
- environments where stop density and pickup discipline are achievable

This is more fundable and more operable than trying to start with broad geography or every product category at once.
