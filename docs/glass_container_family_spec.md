# Glass Container Family Specification

Status: approved for pilot planning

## Purpose

Define the first container families AllDone should prototype around the approved glass-first material system.

## Family A: Storage modules

Rectangular glass modules for chilled food, prepared meals, leftovers, and pantry-adjacent use.

### Proposed initial SKU set

| SKU | Approx. volume | Example outer footprint | Primary use |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Storage-S | 0.6 L | 150 x 100 x 50 mm | single meal, side dish, produce |
| Storage-M | 1.5 L | 150 x 150 x 80 mm | meal prep, salad, prepared food |
| Storage-L | 3.0 L | 300 x 150 x 80 mm | family portion, bulk chilled goods |

### Design rules

- flat, stackable top surface
- rounded interior corners for easier washing
- limited moving parts
- geometry that works on common home shelves
- closure that is robust enough for transport and repeated wash cycles

## Family B: Dose modules

Small cylindrical glass modules for liquids, oils, sauces, cosmetics, hygiene products, and measured consumables.

### Proposed initial SKU set

| SKU | Approx. volume | Example outer size | Primary use |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Dose-S | 50 ml | 30 x 120 mm | samples, concentrates, travel-size use |
| Dose-M | 100 ml | 40 x 110 mm | household consumables, sauces, hygiene |
| Dose-L | 200 ml | 50 x 130 mm | higher-turnover liquids |

### Design rules

- stable base
- straightforward neck and closure standard
- tamper-evident option where needed
- easy inspection for residue and wear

## Pilot decision

The pilot is locked to the middle SKUs:

- Storage-M
- Dose-M

No third pilot SKU is included in pilot one.

These give the best chance of learning quickly without overexpanding tooling and wash complexity.

## Closure decision

### Storage-M

- stainless minimal-part closure
- screw-thread or quarter-turn direction
- removable silicone gasket
- no complex clip-heavy mechanism in pilot one

### Dose-M

- standardized screw-neck closure
- removable silicone gasket or seal
- tamper-evident option where category requires it

## Mechanical targets

- survive repeated depot wash cycles
- survive normal stacking and fridge handling
- tolerate at least low-height drop and impact scenarios better than generic kitchenware
- keep closure replacement simpler than whole-container replacement

## Operational targets

- compatible with rack-based transport
- compatible with visual inspection after wash
- compatible with deposit, subscription, or managed-return loops
- easy to count, sort, and repack

## Remaining open questions for prototyping

- exact wall thickness for the first production-intent samples
- exact neck finish for `Dose-M`
- exact quarter-turn versus screw-thread choice for `Storage-M`
- how much protective secondary transport packaging is required to control breakage
