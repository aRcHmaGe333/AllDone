# Depot Equipment Assumptions

Status: approved for pilot planning

Purpose
-------
This document defines the first depot-equipment assumptions for AllDone.

It closes `P2.4`.

## Core equipment zones

The pilot depot should have:

- dirty intake zone
- quarantine and damaged-hold zone
- wash zone
- drying zone
- inspection and reassembly zone
- refill-ready storage zone
- pack staging zone

## Assumed equipment

### Intake and sorting

- route bins or totes for dirty returns
- counting surface or intake bench
- quarantine crates for damaged units

### Washing

- one commercial dishwash or equivalent small-batch industrial wash path
- racks compatible with the chosen glass container geometry
- separate handling for lids and removable gaskets where needed

### Drying and inspection

- drying racks or clean-air drying surfaces
- inspection bench with strong lighting
- simple replacement-parts bin for gaskets and closures

### Storage and staging

- clean rack storage by SKU
- refill-ready tote or crate system
- route staging area for outbound packed units

## Layout assumptions

- dirty flow and clean flow should not cross
- damaged and quarantined units should be physically separated from refill-ready inventory
- refill-ready inventory should be counted by SKU and state, not stored as undifferentiated stock

## Pilot-scale assumption

The first pilot should assume a small centralized depot footprint rather than a high-automation facility.

The point is operational proof, not industrial-scale capex.
